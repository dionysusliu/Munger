# IFIP

The **International Federation for Information Processing** (IFIP) is a leading international, non-governmental organization dedicated to the advancement of information and communication technology. Founded in 1960 under the auspices of [[UNESCO]], IFIP fosters global cooperation among computing professionals, researchers, and national societies.

## Overview
IFIP supports the information technology community through:
- Organization of international conferences and technical workshops
- Publication of peer-reviewed proceedings and standards
- Promotion of best practices in [[Information Technology]] and [[Computer Science]]
- Facilitation of cross-border academic and industrial collaboration

## Historical Document Excerpt
The following text is preserved from a foundational paper published under IFIP's conference proceedings, addressing early architectural concepts in [[Computer Networking]].

### Abstract
This brief paper offers a perspective on the subject of names of destinations in [[Data Communication Networks]]. It suggests two ideas:
1. It is helpful to distinguish among four different kinds of objects that may be named as the destination of a packet in a network.
2. The [[Operating System]] concept of [[Binding]] is a useful way to describe the relations among the four kinds of objects.

To illustrate the usefulness of this approach, the paper interprets some more subtle and confusing properties of two real-world network systems for naming destinations.
