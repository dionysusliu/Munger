# NW

**NW** is an acronym commonly used in business, project management, and strategic analysis to denote **Net Worth** or **Net Wealth**. It represents the difference between an individual’s or organization’s total assets and total liabilities, serving as a key indicator of financial health and value.

## Definition

**Net Worth** is calculated as:

> **Net Worth = Total Assets – Total Liabilities**

- **Assets** include cash, investments, property, equipment, intellectual property, and accounts receivable.
- **Liabilities** include debts, loans, mortgages, accounts payable, and other financial obligations.

A positive NW indicates that assets exceed liabilities (solvency), while a negative NW suggests insolvency or financial distress.

## Examples

### Personal Finance
- **Example 1:** Alex has $50,000 in savings, a car worth $20,000, and a house valued at $300,000. He owes $180,000 on his mortgage and $5,000 on a credit card.
  - **NW = ($50,000 + $20,000 + $300,000) – ($180,000 + $5,000) = $370,000 – $185,000 = $185,000**

### Business Context
- **Example 2:** A startup has $500,000 in cash, $200,000 in equipment, and $100,000 in accounts receivable. It has $300,000 in loans and $50,000 in unpaid bills.
  - **NW = ($500,000 + $200,000 + $100,000) – ($300,000 + $50,000) = $800,000 – $350,000 = $450,000**

### Negative NW
- **Example 3:** A company has $1 million in assets but $1.5 million in debt.
  - **NW = $1,000,000 – $1,500,000 = –$500,000** (insolvent)

## Related Mental Models

- [[Assets vs. Liabilities]] – The fundamental components of NW; understanding the difference is critical for financial decision-making.
- [[Leverage]] – Using borrowed capital (liabilities) to increase potential returns; high leverage can amplify NW changes.
- [[Sunk Cost Fallacy]] – Irrational attachment to past investments can distort NW assessment; avoid valuing assets based on historical cost alone.
- [[Opportunity Cost]] – The value of the next best alternative foregone; relevant when deciding to liquidate assets to improve NW.
- [[Balance Sheet]] – A financial statement that directly reports NW (as equity/shareholder equity) for a business.
- [[Financial Independence]] – A personal finance goal often measured by achieving a high NW relative to expenses.
- [[Risk-Reward Ratio]] – Evaluating investments that affect NW; higher risk may lead to higher NW growth or loss.

## Applications

- **Personal Wealth Tracking:** Use NW to measure progress toward financial goals.
- **Business Valuation:** Investors assess NW as part of due diligence.
- **Creditworthiness:** Lenders evaluate NW to determine loan eligibility.
- **Strategic Planning:** Companies monitor NW to decide on expansion, debt reduction, or dividend policies.

## Limitations

- NW does not capture liquidity (e.g., assets may be illiquid).
- It is a snapshot in time—values can fluctuate with market conditions.
- Intangible assets (e.g., brand value, human capital) are often excluded.
