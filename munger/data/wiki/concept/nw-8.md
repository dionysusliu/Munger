# NW (Nested Wholes)

## Definition

**NW (Nested Wholes)** is a mental model from systems thinking that describes how every system is simultaneously a whole composed of smaller parts and a part of a larger whole. The acronym “NW” emphasizes that no entity exists in isolation – it is always *nested* within a hierarchy of wholes. Understanding this nested structure helps avoid reductionist thinking (focusing only on parts) and prevents over‑aggregation (ignoring internal dynamics).

## Core Principles

- **Holarchy**: A hierarchy of wholes where each level is autonomous yet embedded in a larger context.
- **Emergence**: Properties at a higher level arise from interactions at lower levels but cannot be predicted by analyzing parts alone.
- **Double‑Role**: Every unit functions as a whole for its sub‑units and as a part for its super‑unit.

## Examples

| Domain          | Example of Nested Wholes |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Biology        | A cell is a whole (organelles inside) and a part of a tissue, which is part of an organ, which is part of an organism. |
| Business       | A team is a whole (individual members) and a part of a department, which is part of a company, which is part of an industry. |
| Software       | A function is a whole (lines of code) and a part of a module, which is part of a service, which is part of an application. |
| Ecology        | A tree is a whole (roots, trunk, leaves) and a part of a forest, which is part of a biome, which is part of Earth’s ecosystem. |

## Related Mental Models

- [[Holarchy]] – The formal structure of nested wholes.
- [[Emergence]] – How new properties arise at each level.
- [[Reductionism]] – The opposite trap of ignoring the whole.
- [[Systems Thinking]] – The broader discipline that uses NW.
- [[Silos]] – A failure to recognize how a unit is a part of a larger whole.

## How to Use This Model

1. **Identify the focal level** – e.g., a team, a cell, a feature.
2. **Look one level down** – understand the parts that compose it.
3. **Look one level up** – understand the larger system it serves.
4. **Repeat** – move two or three levels in both directions when solving problems.

## Common Pitfalls

- Staying at one level (fixing symptoms without understanding context).
- Ignoring interactions between levels (e.g., optimizing a part harms the whole).
- Assuming a whole is independent (it always has constraints from above).
