# Flywheel model

## Overview
The **flywheel model** is a strategic mental model that conceptualizes organizational growth, customer engagement, and product adoption as a self-reinforcing cycle rather than a linear pipeline. Unlike traditional [[Funnel model|funnels]] that treat customers as inputs that eventually exit, the flywheel positions the customer at the center, where satisfaction, advocacy, and feedback continuously fuel momentum. When properly aligned, each rotation compounds value, reduces acquisition costs, and creates sustainable growth with diminishing external effort.

## Core Principles
- **Compounding momentum:** Early inputs build inertia; the system becomes easier to sustain over time.
- **Positive feedback loops:** Outputs from one stage directly strengthen inputs to the next.
- **Friction minimization:** Identifying and removing bottlenecks (e.g., poor onboarding, misaligned KPIs, siloed teams) is critical to maintaining velocity.
- **Customer-centric energy:** Delighted customers become growth multipliers through referrals, reviews, and expanded usage.
- **Systemic alignment:** Incentives, metrics, and cross-functional workflows must all push in the same rotational direction.

## How It Works
A functional flywheel typically moves through three interconnected phases:
1. **Attract/Input:** Deliver consistent value that draws users into the ecosystem.
2. **Engage/Experience:** Provide reliable, high-quality interactions that build trust and habitual usage.
3. **Delight/Advocate:** Exceed expectations so customers naturally refer others, share feedback, or expand their footprint.

The cycle is powered by [[Feedback loop]]s and sustained by aligned [[Incentive structure]]s. When teams are rewarded for long-term retention and friction reduction rather than short-term conversions, the flywheel accelerates organically.

## Examples
- **Amazon:** The canonical business flywheel: broader selection → improved customer experience → increased traffic → more third-party sellers → economies of scale → lower prices → repeat.
- **HubSpot:** Replaced the outbound sales funnel with an inbound flywheel (Attract → Engage → Delight), turning customer success and community into primary growth engines.
- **[[Atlas Dynamics]]:** Founded by [[Alice Arbor]], Atlas Dynamics adopted the flywheel model specifically to improve [[Customer retention]]. By mapping the post-acquisition journey, they identified churn triggers and replaced them with proactive onboarding and success touchpoints. [[Bob Stone]], an external advisor, helped redesign [[Incentive structure|incentive structures]] so that engineering, sales, and support shared retention-based KPIs. This alignment optimized [[Feedback loop|feedback loops]] between user analytics and product iteration, turning support tickets into feature prioritization signals and driving compounding loyalty.

## Applications
- **Customer Success & Retention:** Shift focus from CAC to LTV, NPS, and expansion revenue. Build self-serve resources and community forums that reduce support load while increasing engagement.
- **Product Development:** Use behavioral telemetry and [[Feedback loop]]s to prioritize features that increase activation and reduce time-to-value. Treat churn analysis as input for the next sprint.
- **Organizational Design & Compensation:** Replace departmental silos with cross-functional flywheel metrics. Align bonuses with retention, referral rates, and friction reduction rather than one-time closes.
- **Marketing & Growth:** Move from campaign-driven spikes to ecosystem-driven compounding. Leverage user-generated content, referral programs, and partner integrations.
- **Platform & Marketplace Businesses:** Combine the flywheel with [[Network effects]]: more users → more data/content → better matching/experience → more users.

- [ ] Map the current customer journey and identify where momentum stalls
- [ ] Define 2–3 core metrics that reflect cycle velocity (e.g., activation rate, referral rate, support resolution time)
- [ ] Audit [[Incentive structure|incentive structures]] for misalignment with long-term retention
- [ ] Build closed [[Feedback loop]]s between support, product, and marketing
- [ ] Run small experiments to reduce friction at the weakest stage, measure impact, and scale
