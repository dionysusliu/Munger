# J. Saltzer

His work often bridges concepts from [[operating system]] architecture with [[data communication networks]].

## Notable Publications

### RFC 1498: *On the Naming and Binding of Network Destinations*
- **Publication Body:** [[Network Working Group]]
- **Document Type:** Informational Memo
- **Standardization Status:** Does not specify an [[Internet Standard]]
- **Distribution:** Unlimited

#### Abstract & Core Concepts
This paper presents a conceptual framework for analyzing how destinations are identified and addressed in [[data communication networks]]. Saltzer proposes two foundational ideas:
1. **Destination Object Taxonomy:** Distinguishing among four distinct kinds of objects that may be named as the destination of a network packet.
2. **Binding Semantics:** Adapting the [[operating system]] concept of [[binding]] to model and describe the dynamic relationships, mappings, and lifecycles among the four destination object types.

## Research Themes
- **Naming vs. Addressing:** Exploration of the theoretical and practical separation between human/logical identifiers and machine/routing identifiers.
- **Cross-Domain Abstractions:** Application of traditional OS memory and resource binding models to network layer design.
- **Destination Classification:** Development of a structured taxonomy for packet destination identifiers to improve protocol clarity and implementation.
